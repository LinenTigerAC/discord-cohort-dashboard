Activation

Activated = sent 3+ messages in their first week in the data. This mirrors Discord's own first-day activation benchmark, but measured weekly rather than daily since that's the grain we have — treat it as a related, not identical, number. Also: this can only measure people who sent at least one message, so it's an upper bound on true server-wide activation, not a measure against your full ~430k membership.

One Year in Review — The Turnaround

▸ expand
Full history back to January 2025, corrected for a Statbot export cap that undercounted 26 weeks (marked with *), and flagged for one week of confirmed data loss (marked with †). See the note below the chart for details on both.
The small dots along the bottom of the chart mark real events — ● program launches, ● feature launches, ● milestones, ● leadership/incidents, ● application cycles & game updates. Hover any dot for what it was. Full list in the Event Log at the bottom of the page.
And the depth side of the same story — median messages per active member, per week. Note: for the 26 corrected weeks (*), this line is not corrected the way active-member counts are — we only have an aggregate override for headcount and total volume, not the underlying per-user rows, so the median for those weeks is computed from the partial (capped) data only and should be read as approximate.
Same calendar week, one year apart, active members and depth side by side. Because early-to-mid 2025 was an inflated, viral-driven peak that later collapsed, comparing against it will mostly show a decline on the active-members side — that's the artificial 2025 baseline, not a problem with current performance. The "growth since the trough" numbers above are the fairer read for headcount. Depth tends to move less dramatically, since it's already normalized per person.
